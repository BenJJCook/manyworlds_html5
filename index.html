Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon, in Arizona, USA, is 277 miles long and reaches up to over a mile in depth.

Big Ben

Located in the heart of London, Big Ben is actually the name of the bell atop the clock of the Palace of Westminster.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon rainforest is the largest tropical rainforest in the world, and holds over half of the planet's known species.

Antarctica

Despite how it may seem, Antarctica is actually the driest continent on the planet, and holds the largest desert on Earth: Maudlandia.

Camping

Camping is fun, especially when the stars are out.

Sahara Desert

The Sahara Desert is actually the world's THIRD largest desert, however it is the world's largest HOT desert.

Scotland

The Scottish Highlands may be cold, but their beauty is unrivalled.

Iceland

Unlike Greenland, Iceland has very little ice. Instead, it has breathtaking landscapes, and incredibly blue waters.

Maui

Maui is one of the islands that make up Hawaii. Its beaches are one of the most popular tourist destinations, in the world.

Swiss Alps

When the clouds are low enough, this bizarre sight will emerge atop the incredible mountains of the Swiss Alps.

Japan

Mt. Fuji is the tallest mountain in Japan. Famed for its uniform shape on otherwise level land, seeing it beneath the stars is a rare but mindblowing sight.

Dubai

Dubai is a young yet incredibly modern looking city, and is one of the most popular destinations for young tourists. Unfortunately, it still sits on a very archaic set of laws.
